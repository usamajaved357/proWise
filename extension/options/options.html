How Snag AI works
Everything you need — from first setup to writing proposals that win. Read this once, apply it every session.
Quick Start
Up and running in under 5 minutes
01
Add your email and verify it
Go to Settings → Account Email. Enter the same email you used or plan to use on Paddle. Click Send code — a 6-digit OTP arrives in your inbox. Enter it to verify. This step is required before any proposal can be generated.
→ Settings page
02
Add your Upwork profile URL
Go to Subscription and paste your Upwork profile URL into the slot. Both standard URLs (upwork.com/freelancers/~xxx) and custom profile URLs work. A Sync button appears next to the slot — click it to open your profile in a new tab.
→ Subscription page
03
Click "Sync Profile" on your Upwork profile page
Once your profile page loads, a "Sync Profile" pill button appears in the bottom-right corner of the page. Click it. Snag AI reads your name, JSS %, tier badge, hourly rate, total earnings, skills, bio, employment history, and portfolio items all in one go. The button turns green when done.
→ On your Upwork profile page
04
Add live URLs to portfolio items, then open any job
Go to Profiles → Portfolio. Items with a green dot already have URLs and will be referenced in proposals. Items with an amber dot are missing a URL — click ··· → Edit to add one. Then open any Upwork job and click Write Proposal.
→ Profiles page
Profile & Portfolio Setup
The data behind every proposal
↺
What the "Sync Profile" button reads
Syncing is not automatic — it happens when you click the "Sync Profile" pill that appears in the bottom-right corner of your Upwork profile page. One click reads everything in one pass: name, professional title, JSS %, hourly rate, tier badge, total earnings, jobs completed, hours worked, skills list, bio, employment history, education, languages, and all portfolio items.

To re-sync after changes on Upwork, open your profile page again (use the ⟳ Sync button on the profile card in the Profiles page) and click the pill again.
◈
Portfolio items — green dot vs amber dot Important
After syncing, open Profiles → Portfolio. Each item shows a dot in the top-left of its card.

A green dot means the item has a live URL — the AI selects the most relevant ones per job and links to them directly in the proposal.
An amber dot means no URL is attached — the AI knows the project exists but cannot reference it with a link, which significantly reduces how useful it is as evidence.

Click ··· → Edit on any amber-dot item to add a live URL.
⊞
Multiple profiles Pro & Agency
Pro supports up to 3 Upwork profiles. Agency supports 5. Add each profile URL in the Subscription page. In Profiles, click Set primary on the profile you want to use as the default. You can switch the primary at any time without re-syncing.
!
Re-sync after Upwork profile changes. If you update your title, skills, tier, or add new portfolio items on Upwork, visit your profile page again so Snag AI picks up the latest data. Proposals use whatever was last synced.
Settings
Control how proposals sound and how long they run
T
Tone
Three options control the voice of every proposal.

Conversational — warm, direct, reads like a message from someone who knows their craft without sounding corporate. Best for most jobs.
Professional — more formal structure, better suited for enterprise clients or agency-facing roles.
Aggressive — bold, confident, no hedging. Good for competitive niches where you need to stand out hard.
↔
Length
Controls the target word count the AI aims for.

Tight (75–100 words) — hook, one proof point, CTA. Best for high-volume applying or when proposals are already competitive.
Standard (130–160 words) — hook, context, portfolio reference, scope note, CTA. The default for most situations.
Detailed (160–200 words) — adds more background, good for complex or technical jobs where the client is evaluating depth. Use sparingly — shorter usually converts better.
◷
Freelancer Type
Tells the AI your field so it can tailor how it describes your work and which portfolio items it prioritises. Options: Developer, Designer, Writer, Marketer, VA / Admin, Other.

All types use the same core proposal structure. The difference is in how the AI phrases your experience, which signals it picks up on from the job description, and how it handles pricing and timelines. Set this once and it applies to every proposal you generate.
→
Settings apply immediately after clicking Save settings. Refinements use the same settings as the original proposal — changing settings mid-refinement has no effect until the next fresh generation.
Subscription & Billing
How your plan and proposal quota work
!
Your email in Settings must match your Paddle purchase email. This is how Snag AI links your subscription to your account and unlocks your proposal quota. If the emails don't match, you'll be treated as a free user regardless of payment.
◷
When proposals reset
Your quota resets on your exact subscription anniversary date — not the 1st of each month. If you subscribed on June 15, proposals reset on July 15. The exact reset date is shown in Subscription → Next billing date. Refinements (changing an existing proposal) do not count against your quota — only fresh generations do.
↑
Upgrading or downgrading
Go to Subscription → Upgrade your plan and select a different tier. If you already have an active subscription, Snag AI modifies it in place — no second subscription is created. Paddle prorates the difference and charges or credits immediately.
×
Cancellation — access continues until period ends
Cancelling stops the next charge but does not cut off access immediately. Your plan stays active until the current billing period ends. The billing card shows an amber "Cancels [date]" notice. After expiry you revert to free (2 proposals total).
⊞
Invoices and payment method
Click Manage billing in the Subscription page. This opens your Paddle customer portal where you can update your card, download invoices, and reactivate a cancelled subscription.
Job Analysis & Filters
Know before you spend a Connect
◑
The job alert — two scores, one factor grid
When you open a job, Snag AI reads live data from Upwork's internal store — proposal count, interviews in progress, client hire rate, payment status, client spend, and more — then shows two scores side by side.

Win Probability — how competitive is this slot right now. Based on time since posting, how many proposals are in, how many interviews are active, and whether the client is a reliable hirer.
Profile Match — how well your profile fits this specific job. Based on skill overlap, your JSS vs the job's minimum, your rate vs the listed budget, and any tier or location requirements.

Below the scores, every factor that went into both scores is listed with its individual contribution — positive factors in green, negatives in red. This shows exactly what's working for you on this job and what isn't.
≡
Job Filters — set your own thresholds
In the Profiles page, expand Job Filters under any profile. Start with a preset, then adjust individual controls.

Conservative — tight limits: max 20 proposals, max 1 interviewing, min 50% hire rate, min $1K client spend, payment verification required, min 70 alert score. Best for protecting Connects on a limited budget.
Balanced — the default. Sensible thresholds that catch obvious bad fits without blocking borderline-good jobs.
Aggressive — minimal filtering, min 40 alert score, auto-skip off. You see nearly everything and decide manually.

The controls are grouped into five sections:
— Competition: max proposals in, max freelancers interviewing, max invites already sent
— Client Quality: min client rating, min hire rate, min total spent (dropdown), warn on $0 clients, payment verified toggle
— Rate, Age & Match: max rate mismatch %, max job age in days, min skill match %
— Job Requirements: warn on location filters, warn on tier requirements
— Alert Behaviour: min combined score to show the alert screen, auto-skip already-hired jobs toggle
Signals that score negatively — red is a strong skip, amber means weigh it up
Job already has a hire — position is filled
50+ proposals already submitted
Client rating below 4.0 stars
Payment method not verified
Client has $0 total spend on Upwork
Client hire rate below 10%
3 or more freelancers already interviewing
Posted 3+ days ago with active interviews
Job requires a location you don't match
Your JSS is below the job's minimum requirement
→
The alert is a guide, not a gatekeeper. Any job can still be applied to — click Write Proposal regardless of what the scores show. Use the factor breakdown to decide whether this one is worth the Connects.
AI Proposal Writing
What happens when you click Write Proposal
⊳
7 opening hooks — one selected per job
Every proposal opens with one of 7 hook patterns. Snag AI picks which one fits based on the job type (fixed vs hourly), budget size, duration, and client signals — not randomly. Each hook is designed for a specific situation.
Hook 1 — Proof
Lead with a specific result
"I delivered X for [client type] — here's how I'd do the same for you." Uses your most relevant portfolio project. Best when you have a direct match.
Hook 2 — Relatability
I've done this exact type of work
"I've handled [specific work type] across [N] projects — here's how I'd approach yours." Good for ongoing or part-time roles where experience rhythm matters.
Hook 3 — Guarantee
Price and timeline stated upfront
"I can deliver this in [X weeks] at [price], backed by [portfolio evidence]." Used on fixed-price jobs where clients want certainty fast.
Hook 4 — Extra Value
I'll do more than asked
"I'll handle the brief — and also [extra thing they haven't asked for but need]." Differentiates from freelancers who only answer the literal question.
Hook 5 — Call
Invite a quick call right now
"Let's jump on a 15-minute call — I'll walk you through exactly how I'd approach this." Works well for jobs where trust or scope clarity matters more than price.
Hook 6 — Numbers
Lead with your track record stats
"Here's my record: [N] projects delivered, [JSS]% success rate, [X] hours/week available." Works when client-side filters and social proof matter more than story.
Hook 7 — Client First
Restate their need better than they wrote it
"My read of what you actually need: [restatement that shows deeper understanding than the brief]." Used on large-budget or complex jobs where showing comprehension is the differentiator.
◎
Screening keywords — detected and included automatically
Many clients hide a test phrase in the job description — things like "include the word 'pineapple' in your reply." Snag AI reads the full description before generating and includes the required word naturally in the proposal. You will never fail a hidden screening test.
◐
Right panel — live job intelligence while you write
Once a proposal is generated, the right panel shows three things:

Win Probability and Profile Match scores — same as the alert, refreshed for the current job so you know where you stand.
Writing Tips — contextual, computed notes based on the job data. Examples: if the client is in a different timezone, a tip suggests mentioning your overlap hours; if their budget is below your rate, a tip flags it and suggests how to address it in the letter.
Job Signals — AI-generated observations about this specific job, drawn from the job description and client history. These are short notes on anything notable that could be worked into your proposal.
↻
Refine without regenerating the full proposal
Four quick-action chips appear below the proposal: Shorter, More confident, Add my rate, Adjust for budget. Clicking one immediately sends the current proposal back to the AI with that specific instruction — only that part changes.

You can also type any custom instruction in the text box: "mention their React Native requirement", "cut the second paragraph", "add a Loom offer". Press Cmd Enter (Mac) or Ctrl Enter (Windows) or click Regenerate.

Refinements do not count against your monthly proposal quota.
✎
Live editing and word count
The proposal text box is fully editable — click anywhere and type. Make quick tweaks before copying without going through a regeneration. The word count indicator in the top-right of the proposal box turns amber above 120 words and red above 160 words. Shorter proposals have meaningfully higher read rates on Upwork.
Tips for Winning More Jobs
What actually makes the difference
—
Apply within the first hour
Being among the first 5 proposals gives a significant advantage. Jobs with fewer than 5 submissions and posted under 1 hour score highest in the Win Probability calculation. Speed matters more than perfection at this stage.
—
Portfolio URLs are what make the proof real
The AI references your portfolio projects by name and links to them in the proposal. Without a live URL, it can mention the project but cannot prove it exists. A proposal with two verified links reads completely differently from one without any.
—
Shorter almost always wins
Clients decide in the first 160 characters whether to read further. Keep proposals under 130 words unless the job explicitly asks for detailed answers. Use the Shorter chip when a proposal runs long — it keeps the hook and evidence and cuts the rest.
—
Use the refine box for one specific thing
The most effective refinements are narrow: "mention their Python background", "cut the last sentence", "make the opening more direct". Vague instructions like "make it better" rarely improve the letter — specific ones almost always do.
—
Unverified payment and 0% hire rate are hard no's
Clients with no payment method on file or who have never hired from any of their posted jobs almost never result in a hire. The alert flags these — take the flag seriously and move on.
—
Keep your profile synced after Upwork changes
If you earn a new badge, update your rate, or add portfolio items on Upwork, re-visit your profile page and re-sync portfolios. Proposals use stale data silently — there's no warning if your sync is old.
→
The compound effect: Apply early + relevant portfolio links + tight word count + one specific refinement = consistently higher response rates. Each factor adds a marginal edge — all four together is a meaningfully different proposal.
Portfolio Links — by Category
What kind of URL to use for your field

Snag AI selects the most relevant portfolio items for each job and references them by name in the proposal. This only works when items have live URLs. Items with a green dot in the Profiles page are used; amber dots are skipped. Below is what counts as a good URL per category.

—
Developer
Link to the live product: App Store listing, Play Store listing, web app URL, or GitHub repo. Avoid linking to a single code file — link where a client can see or use the thing you built.
—
Designer
Use Behance, Dribbble, or Figma Community links, or a hosted case study page. A specific project page converts better than a generic portfolio homepage — link directly to the work.
—
Writer / Copywriter
Link to live sales pages, published articles, or landing pages. Medium, Substack, and client websites work well. Avoid Google Docs — they often require login and clients won't bother.
—
Marketer / VA
Link to case study pages or campaign result summaries. If the results are confidential, a stripped-down overview page without client names is still better than no link at all.
→
To add a URL: go to Profiles → Portfolio, click ··· on any item, then Edit. Paste the live link and save. The dot on the card updates immediately.
