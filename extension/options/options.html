How Snag AI works
Everything you need — from first setup to writing proposals that win. Read this once, apply it every session.
Quick Start — Up and running in 5 minutes

01 — Add your email and verify it
Go to Settings → Account Email. Enter the same email you used or plan to use on Paddle. Click Send code — a 6-digit OTP arrives in your inbox. Enter it to verify. This step is required before any proposal can be generated.

02 — Add your Upwork profile URL
Go to Subscription and paste your Upwork profile URL into the slot. Both standard URLs (upwork.com/freelancers/~xxx) and custom profile URLs work. A Sync button appears next to the slot — click it to open your profile in a new tab.

03 — Click "Sync Profile" on your Upwork profile page
Once your profile page loads, a "Sync Profile" pill button appears in the bottom-right corner. Click it. Snag AI reads your name, JSS %, tier badge, hourly rate, total earnings, skills, bio, employment history, and portfolio items all in one pass. The button turns green when done.

04 — Add live URLs to portfolio items, then open any job
Go to Profiles → Portfolio. Items with a green dot already have URLs and will be referenced in proposals. Items with an amber dot are missing a URL — click ··· → Edit to add one. Then open any Upwork job and click Write Proposal.

Profile & Portfolio Setup

Syncing is not automatic — it happens when you click the "Sync Profile" pill that appears in the bottom-right corner of your Upwork profile page. One click reads everything in one pass: name, professional title, JSS %, hourly rate, tier badge, total earnings, jobs completed, hours worked, skills list, bio, employment history, education, languages, and all portfolio items.

To re-sync after changes on Upwork, open your profile page again using the ⟳ Sync button on the profile card in the Profiles page, then click the pill again.

After syncing, open Profiles → Portfolio. Each item shows a dot in the top-left of its card. A green dot means the item has a live URL — the AI selects the most relevant ones per job and links to them directly in the proposal. An amber dot means no URL is attached — the AI knows the project exists but cannot reference it with a link, which significantly reduces how useful it is as evidence.

Click ··· → Edit on any amber-dot item to add a live URL. Re-sync after Upwork profile changes. If you update your title, skills, tier, or add new portfolio items on Upwork, visit your profile page again so Snag AI picks up the latest data. Proposals use whatever was last synced.

Pro supports up to 3 Upwork profiles. Agency supports 5. Add each profile URL in the Subscription page. In Profiles, click Set primary on the profile you want to use as the default for proposals. You can switch the primary at any time without re-syncing.

Settings

Tone — Controls the voice of every proposal. Conversational is warm and direct, best for most jobs. Professional uses a more formal structure, better for enterprise clients or agency roles. Aggressive is bold with no hedging, good for competitive niches where you need to stand out hard.

Length — Controls the target word count. Tight (75–100 words) is hook, one proof point, CTA — best for high-volume applying. Standard (130–160 words) adds context, portfolio reference, scope note, and CTA — the default for most situations. Detailed (160–200 words) adds more depth for complex technical jobs. Shorter usually converts better.

Freelancer Type — Tells the AI your field so it can tailor how it describes your work and which portfolio items it prioritises. Options: Developer, Designer, Writer, Marketer, VA / Admin, Other. Set this once and it applies to every proposal. All types use the same core proposal structure — the difference is in how the AI phrases your experience and picks up on job signals.

Settings apply immediately after clicking Save. Refinements use the same settings as the original proposal — changing settings mid-refinement has no effect until the next fresh generation.

Subscription & Billing

Your email in Settings must match your Paddle purchase email. This is how Snag AI links your subscription to your account and unlocks your proposal quota. If the emails don't match, you'll be treated as a free user regardless of payment.

Your quota resets on your exact subscription anniversary date — not the 1st of each month. If you subscribed on June 15, proposals reset on July 15. The exact reset date is shown in Subscription → Next billing date. Refinements (changing an existing proposal) do not count against your quota — only fresh generations do.

To upgrade or downgrade, go to Subscription → Upgrade your plan and select a different tier. Snag AI modifies your existing subscription in place — no second subscription is created. Paddle prorates the difference immediately.

Cancelling stops the next charge but does not cut off access immediately. Your plan stays active until the current billing period ends. The billing card shows an amber "Cancels [date]" notice. After expiry you revert to the free plan (2 proposals total). Click Manage billing to update your card, view invoices, or reactivate.

Job Analysis & Filters

When you open a job, Snag AI reads live data from Upwork's internal store — proposal count, interviews in progress, client hire rate, payment status, client spend, and more — then shows two scores side by side.

Win Probability — How competitive is this slot right now. Based on time since posting, how many proposals are in, how many interviews are active, and whether the client is a reliable hirer.

Profile Match — How well your profile fits this specific job. Based on skill overlap, your JSS vs the job's minimum, your rate vs the listed budget, and any tier or location requirements.

Below the scores, every factor that went into both scores is listed with its individual contribution. Red factors mean skip or think twice, green factors confirm a good fit.

In Profiles → Job Filters, three presets are available. Conservative is for protecting Connects — tight limits on proposal count, hire rate, and minimum score. Balanced is the default with sensible thresholds. Aggressive applies minimal filtering so you decide manually. Individual sliders let you fine-tune: max proposals, max interviewing, min hire rate, min client rating, min client spend, rate mismatch tolerance, job age, and skill match. Toggle auto-skip if hired to dismiss closed listings without a prompt. The alert is a guide, not a gatekeeper — click Write Proposal on any job regardless of what the scores show.

AI Proposal Writing

Every proposal opens with one of 7 hook patterns. Snag AI picks which one fits based on the job type (fixed vs hourly), budget size, duration, and client signals — not randomly.

Hook 1 — Proof: Lead with a specific result from your portfolio. Uses your most relevant project. Best when you have a direct match.

Hook 2 — Relatability: "I've handled this exact type of work across N projects." Good for ongoing or part-time roles where experience rhythm matters.

Hook 3 — Guarantee: Price and timeline stated upfront. Used on fixed-price jobs where clients want certainty fast.

Hook 4 — Extra Value: "I'll handle the brief — and also [extra thing they haven't asked for but need]." Differentiates from freelancers who only answer the literal question.

Hook 5 — Call: Invite a 15-minute call right now. Works well for jobs where trust or scope clarity matters more than price.

Hook 6 — Numbers: Lead with your track record stats — JSS %, jobs delivered, hours available. Works when client-side filters and social proof matter.

Hook 7 — Client First: Restate their need better than they wrote it. Used on large-budget or complex jobs where showing comprehension is the differentiator.

Screening keywords — Many clients hide a test phrase in the job description ("include the word 'pineapple' in your reply"). Snag AI reads the full description before generating and includes the required word naturally. You will never fail a hidden screening test.

Right panel while writing — Win Probability and Profile Match scores refresh for the current job. Writing Tips show contextual notes based on job data (timezone overlap, budget mismatch, competition level, skill gap). Job Signals are AI-generated observations about this specific job drawn from the description and client history.

Refining proposals — Four quick chips appear below the proposal: Shorter, More confident, Add my rate, Adjust for budget. Clicking one immediately sends the current proposal back to the AI with that specific instruction. You can also type any custom instruction in the text box. Press Cmd Enter (Mac) or Ctrl Enter (Windows) or click Regenerate. Refinements do not count against your monthly proposal quota.

Live editing — The proposal text box is fully editable. The word count indicator turns amber above 120 words and red above 160 words. Shorter proposals have meaningfully higher read rates on Upwork.

Portfolio Links — by Category

Snag AI selects the most relevant portfolio items for each job and references them by name in the proposal. This only works when items have live URLs. Items with a green dot are used; amber dots are skipped.

Developer — Link to the live product: App Store listing, Play Store listing, web app URL, or GitHub repo. Avoid linking to a single code file — link where a client can see or use the thing you built.

Designer — Use Behance, Dribbble, or Figma Community links, or a hosted case study page. A specific project page converts better than a generic portfolio homepage.

Writer / Copywriter — Link to live sales pages, published articles, or landing pages. Medium, Substack, and client websites work well. Avoid Google Docs — they often require login and clients won't bother.

Marketer / VA — Link to case study pages or campaign result summaries. If the results are confidential, a stripped-down overview page without client names is still better than no link at all.

To add a URL: go to Profiles → Portfolio, click ··· on any item, then Edit. Paste the live link and save. The dot on the card updates immediately.

Tips for Winning More Jobs

Apply within the first hour — Being among the first 5 proposals gives a significant advantage. Jobs with fewer than 5 submissions and posted under 1 hour score highest in Win Probability. Speed matters more than perfection at this stage.

Portfolio URLs are what make the proof real — The AI references your portfolio projects by name and links to them in the proposal. Without a live URL, it can mention the project but cannot prove it exists. A proposal with two verified links reads completely differently from one without any.

Shorter almost always wins — Clients decide in the first 160 characters whether to read further. Keep proposals under 130 words unless the job explicitly asks for detailed answers. Use the Shorter chip when a proposal runs long.

Use the refine box for one specific thing — The most effective refinements are narrow: "mention their Python background", "cut the last sentence", "make the opening more direct". Vague instructions like "make it better" rarely improve the letter — specific ones almost always do.

Unverified payment and 0% hire rate are hard no's — Clients with no payment method on file or who have never hired from any of their posted jobs almost never result in a hire. The alert flags these — take the flag seriously and move on.

Keep your profile synced after Upwork changes — If you earn a new badge, update your rate, or add portfolio items on Upwork, re-visit your profile page and click Sync Profile again. Proposals use stale data silently — there's no warning if your sync is old.

New subscriber guarantee — If you subscribed within the last 7 days and have generated fewer than 5 proposals, you may request a full refund without explanation. We will process your refund within 5 business days without any questions.

Technical failure — If the Service was materially unavailable or broken due to issues on our end for a significant portion of your billing period (48+ hours of continuous unavailability), you may request a pro-rated refund for the affected period. We will review each case fairly and respond within 3 business days.

Billing errors — If you were charged incorrectly — for example, charged at the wrong rate, charged twice, or charged after a confirmed cancellation — you are entitled to a full refund of the incorrect amount. Contact us immediately at support@snagai.app and we will resolve the error within 5 business days.

Non-Refundable Circumstances

Unless required by applicable statutory law, refunds will not be issued in the following circumstances:

— Change of mind after actively using the Service (generating proposals).

— Dissatisfaction with AI output quality, writing style, or proposal effectiveness.

— Failure to cancel before an auto-renewal date after the statutory withdrawal period has expired.

— Account suspension or termination due to violation of our Terms of Service.

— Proposal quota already consumed during the billing period.

— Requests made more than 30 days after the charge date (outside statutory periods).

We reserve the right to refuse refund requests that appear to abuse this policy, such as repeated refund requests across multiple subscription periods.

Cancellations

You may cancel your Snag AI subscription at any time from the Subscription page in the extension by clicking "Manage Billing" and following the prompts in the Paddle customer portal. Cancellation stops future automatic charges but does not immediately terminate your access — you retain full access to your plan's features until the end of your current paid billing period. No refund is issued for the unused portion of the current period upon cancellation, except where required by law.

After cancellation, your account automatically reverts to the free plan (limited to 2 lifetime proposals). Your synced profile data remains stored locally in your browser and is not deleted by cancellation.

How to Request a Refund

To request a refund, email us at support@snagai.app with the following information:

— The email address associated with your Snag AI account.

— The approximate date of the charge you are disputing.

— A brief description of the reason for your refund request.

We aim to respond to all refund requests within 1–2 business days. Once approved, refunds are processed by Paddle and returned to your original payment method. Processing times vary by bank and payment method — typically 5–10 business days, but may take up to 15 business days in some cases.

You may also contact Paddle's buyer support directly at paddle.net/contact if you have questions about a charge or wish to initiate a refund request directly with the Merchant of Record.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Refund Policy from time to time. Any changes will be effective from the date of publication. Material changes that affect your refund rights will be communicated via the extension or by email with reasonable notice before taking effect.
